**Post-traumatic stress** occurs when the nervous system stays on alert long after a threatening event (or series of events). Flashbacks, hypervigilance, nightmares, avoidance, intense body sensations: the past invades the present.

**EMDR therapy** is one of the most documented approaches for this kind of suffering. It aims to help the brain **reprocess** what has remained “stuck”.

## What happens in post-traumatic stress

During a shock, survival systems (fight, flight, freeze) can interrupt normal processing. The memory stays emotionally and physiologically charged, as if the danger never ended.

This is not a lack of willpower. Understanding the [link between memory and trauma](/en/memoire-traumatisme-guerison/) often helps reduce guilt and make sense of therapy.

## How EMDR works with post-traumatic stress

The **EMDR method** uses bilateral stimulation (eye movements, sounds, tapping) while a memory or sensation linked to the trauma is carefully recalled.

In a safe framework, the work aims to:

- lower the emotional charge of the memory
- place the event back in the past (autobiographical memory)
- reduce automatic survival reactions
- restore an inner sense of safety

Trauma is not “erased”: it is **digested**. Full overview: [What is EMDR therapy?](/en/therapie-emdr/).

## A natural process, not a magic wand

EMDR does not force the memory or take control for you. It relies on the brain’s capacity to process information when safety conditions are met. More on this in [EMDR as a natural process](/en/emdr-traitement-trauma-processus-naturel/).
